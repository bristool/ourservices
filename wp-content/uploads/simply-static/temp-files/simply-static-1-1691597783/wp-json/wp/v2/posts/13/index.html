Colombia captain Catalina Usme is \u201cdreaming big\u201d and coach Nelson Abadia wants to \u201cmake history\u201d as the last team standing from the Americas plots the downfall of England in the Women\u2019s World Cup quarter-finals.<\/p>\n

The 25th-ranked South Americans have never made the last eight before but they are brimming with confidence after grinding down Jamaica 1-0 on Tuesday thanks to a goal from their skipper.<\/p>\n

They will now play England in Sydney on Saturday and are fearless, having beaten world number two Germany in the group stage.<\/p>\n

\u201cBecause of their football and their history we need to be careful, we need to be cautious,\u201d Abadia said of Sarina Wiegman\u2019s strongly fancied England.<\/p>\n

\u201cBut in football we have already seen that the gaps between teams have been closing down and Colombia have proven to be a solid team.<\/p>\n

\u201cOf course, England are one of the favourites, that goes without saying – they are the European champions.<\/p>\n

\u201cBut we also faced Germany, who were second favourites. We were wise enough and had the composure.\u201d<\/p>\n

Colombia\u2019s intense, attacking mindset has served them well so far, with their teenage striker Linda Caicedo one of the stars of the tournament.<\/p>\n

But Usme is the glue that keeps them together and she said they were in no mood to stop now.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe want more,\u201d she said, having scored the goal to take Colombia into the last eight.<\/p>\n

\u201cIt\u2019s not our ceiling, now we are thinking about England, which is going to be a dream match.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe need to be calm, play an intelligent match, but with the certainty that we can achieve more in this World Cup.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe are dreaming big – we can do this,\u201d she added.
\nAbadia has been in charge since 2017 and after failing to qualify for the last tournament in France, has nurtured the likes of Caicedo into the players they are today.<\/p>\n

The 67-year-old has made no secret of their intention to at least reach the final and match the feat of Brazil in 2007, when they were runners-up to Germany.<\/p>\n

No South American team has won the Women\u2019s World Cup.<\/p>\n

\u201cWhen we qualified for the World Cup the first thing I said to my team is that we are not here just to spend some time – we want to make history and it is better to make history than to tell history,\u201d he said.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe have consolidated a very strong group, both for the technical staff and for the players.<\/p>\n
